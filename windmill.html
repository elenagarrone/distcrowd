As the raise has met its tipping point target of £1,250,000, the Directors of E5 Energy Ltd decided to extend the fundraising date to May 31, 2015 to allow additional investment into the next tranches. Those who lent to the first tranche will start earning interest from May 12th. For more on why we are raising money in tranches, go to "more about the project", below.

Trillion Fund is offering a chance to make a fixed 7 per cent a year for three years through this sustainable, peer-to-peer loan to UK wind energy.

The minimum amount, also known as the Tipping Point, of £1.25m was reached four days early on April 27, so the raise has now moved into the second phase with smaller tranches of £250,000 each. For every further tranche of £250,000 that is filled, another turbine is added to the security package, up to a maximum of ten turbines and £2.5 million.

Investors’ money will be secured against fully functioning, single installation wind turbines located across the UK, built by Endurance Wind Power, a global wind manufacturer, and operated and maintained by Earthmill, a leading UK installer, through a venture called E5 Energy.

Together, the ten 50kW turbines that your money will be secured against generate enough energy annually to power more than 500 homes (1,673,446 kWh), although the energy generated by these turbines will be used to power farms and local businesses, with any remaining energy sold to the grid.

The money raised will be used by E5 Energy to refinance loans outstanding to Endurance Wind Power and Earthmill, who will in turn use this to install ten more sites that already have planning approval.

When is the interest paid?

The interest will begin accruing on October 1, the day after the raise has closed. The first interest payment will be March 31.

How are Endurance and Earthmill able to fix the interest rate?

The companies have predicted the amount of revenue that the five turbines will generate, on average, over the term of the next three years. Taking into account other costs that have to be paid, such as ground rent, maintenance, insurance and Trillion Fund’s fee, the company can calculate what will be left over to service the interest to lenders with a good degree of comfort.

How can returns be predicted if wind speeds vary?

Wind speed does vary – there are windy days and not so windy days; windy months and not so windy months. The annual mean wind speed (AMWS) has been estimated based on data from the NCIC (National Climate Information Centre) and from Earthmill’s installed fleet of 175 turbines across the UK. All turbines are already operational, so estimates shown in the model are based on a combination of design and measured data.

Will I still get my interest and capital repaid if the turbines do not generate as much energy as expected?

The company is obliged to pay interest to lenders. If revenue drops or costs rise, this will firstly eat into profits and would only affect the ability of Endurance and Earthmill to meet the loan repayments if the amount of cash generated by the business fell to below the level required to repay the debt.
